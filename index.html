Business Objective

This dashboard is designed to identify which buildings should be prioritised for renewal based on condition, backlog exposure, and portfolio impact. It supports asset performance review, lifecycle planning, and capital prioritisation in an institutional property setting.

Key Insights

Concentration of renewal demand

The top 10% of buildings by priority score account for over 40% of total renewal need. A targeted intervention strategy focused on the highest-scoring assets could deliver disproportionate portfolio improvement.

Condition–priority misalignment

Several assets categorised as Fair condition carry higher priority scores than some Poor-rated buildings, driven by large capital renewal needs and backlog intensity. Condition alone is insufficient for prioritisation.

Front-loaded renewal pressure

Nearly half of the total renewal need is concentrated within the next five years. This indicates a significant proportion of the estate is approaching or has exceeded its effective service life, requiring immediate capital allocation.

Data & Method

The dashboard uses a cleaned facility dataset with condition, FCI, area, backlog, and renewal-related fields, then derives priority-oriented metrics such as risk band, total renewal need, estimated renewal timing, and recommended action. Priority score combines condition, renewal need, and portfolio impact to support indicative ranking. The portfolio has been adapted into a university estate scenario for analytical demonstration purposes.

Derived fields

  • Priority Score — weighted composite: FCI (35%), condition band (30%), backlog intensity (20%), asset age (15%)
  • Risk Band — High (score ≥ 55), Medium (score 25–54), Low (score < 25)
  • Total Renewal Need — capital renewal requirements + deferred maintenance backlog
  • Backlog per sqm — total renewal need normalised by gross floor area
  • Estimated Renewal Year — modelled from risk band and remaining useful life
  • CRV — derived from FCI and capital requirements; sqft-based fallback for incomplete records

Asset categorisation

  • Buildings grouped into five university-relevant categories: teaching, admin, labs, student facilities, and sports
  • Age bands: Modern (<10 yrs), New (10–20), Mid-life (21–30), Aging (31–50), Mature (51–75), Heritage (>75)
  • Stakeholder ownership mapped to university organisational units
