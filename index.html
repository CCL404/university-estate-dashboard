Key Insights

Concentration of renewal demand

The top 10% of buildings by priority score account for over 40% of total renewal need. Targeted intervention on the highest-scoring assets could deliver disproportionate portfolio improvement.

Condition–priority misalignment

Several Fair-condition assets carry higher priority scores than Poor-rated buildings, driven by large capital renewal needs and backlog intensity. Condition alone is insufficient for prioritisation.

Front-loaded renewal pressure

Nearly half of total renewal need is concentrated within five years, indicating a significant portion of the estate is approaching or has exceeded effective service life.

Data & Method

Note This dashboard uses synthesised portfolio data for demonstration purposes. Metrics, building names, and financial figures do not represent any real institution. The analytical methodology — FCI-based condition profiling, backlog intensity scoring, composite priority modelling, and renewal forecasting — is designed to be transferable to live asset datasets. Built with Chart.js · data processed in Python.
Data source Cleaned facility dataset with condition, FCI, area, backlog, and renewal-related fields, adapted into a university estate scenario for demonstration.
Derived metrics Priority score (FCI + condition + backlog + age), risk band, total renewal need, backlog per sqm, estimated renewal year, and recommended action.
Scenario assumptions Portfolio reclassified into university categories. Priority scoring weights are illustrative. Renewal timing assumes linear deterioration.
